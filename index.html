Reporting Guidelines

How to Report a Hazard

  1. Identify the Hazard: Look for anything that could cause injury or harm in the workplace. This includes physical dangers, chemical exposure, and equipment issues.
  2. Document the Details: Note the exact location, time, and specific conditions surrounding the hazard.
  3. Assess the Severity: Determine if the hazard poses an immediate threat (Critical), significant risk (High), moderate concern (Medium), or minor issue (Low).
  4. Describe the Impact: Explain who could be affected and what injuries or incidents could result.
  5. Submit the Report: Use the MineGuard reporting form with all relevant information.
  6. Follow Up: Monitor your report status through the dashboard and provide additional information if requested.

Hazard Categories

  • Physical Hazards: Falls, crushing, impacts, etc.
  • Chemical Exposure: Toxic gases, skin irritants, etc.
  • Biological Hazards: Infections, contamination, etc.
  • Ergonomic Issues: Repetitive strain, poor posture, etc.
  • Environmental Hazards: Noise, heat, radiation, etc.
  • Equipment Malfunctions: Broken machinery, faulty tools, etc.

Best Practices

  • Report hazards as soon as they are discovered
  • Provide specific, detailed information
  • Include witness information if applicable
  • Suggest corrective actions if you have ideas
  • Follow up on unresolved reports
  • Do not endanger yourself when reporting
